    Computer Science as a discipline

    Computer science is a systematic study of automated computing that spans from the range of theory through programming and mathematics in order to developing computing solutions [1]

    The works of a computer scientist falls into 3 categories

    Designing and building software
    Creating solutions to solve computing problems effectively
    Devising better ways of using computers or AIs

    The process of theory involves the creation of formal definitions, formulating theorems, and developing proofs to verify software and hardware, analyze the performance of specific designs, and determine the theoretical limits of computation.

    The process of abstraction involves recognizing patterns in comparable situations while filtering out information into manageable components only to focus in essential or relevant details

    The process of design is deeply interrelated with theory and abstraction. It aims for better comprehension of the nature of computing through the systematic planning along with creating software to solve computing problems

    Computing Disciplines and Majors

    Computing is an ancient activity that started from counting and calculation which was eventually adopted to many scientific and mathematical innovations. The 5 computing disciplines are:

    Computer Engineering

    Computer engineering is a discipline that embodies the science and technology involved in designing, constructing, implementing, and maintaining the software and hardware components of contemporary computing systems and computer-controlled devices.

    • Studies the design of both digital hardware and software systems.
    • Focuses on digital devices and human computer interaction.
    Computer Science

    Computer Science is a discipline that primarily concentrates on the theoretical aspects of science and digital systems, without placing emphasis on the skillset needed for designing and developing software that fulfills users' needs and requirements.

    • Intellectual process of discipline that combines theory, abstraction, and design
    • Focuses more on the theoretical aspects of computers and computational systems
    • It involves working with mathematical models, data analysis and security, algorithms, and computational theory.
    Information Systems

    Information Systems is a discipline that focuses on the study of the application of information technology in organizations to implement or support business processes and organizational efficiency

    • Serves as a bridge between the technical and management communities within an organization
    • It highlights the importance of information and views technology as a tool to facilitate the creation, processing, and distribution of necessary information.
    Information Technology

    Information Technology is a discipline that focuses more on the application of technology that meets the technological needs of business, government, healthcare, and other kinds of organizations.

    • Emphasizes on the technology itself rather that the information it conveys
    • IT specialists assume responsibility in selecting both the hardware and software products appropriate for an organization along with integrating, installing, customizing, and maintaining those applications for the organization’s users
    Software Engineering

    Software Engineering is the discipline of developing and maintaining software systems that behaves efficiently and reliably and affordable to maintain

    • It is an application of engineering that approaches the development of software and is concerned with the research, evaluation, analysis, design, construction, implementation, and management of software systems
